Hydrogen: Another Chapter in ExxonMobil’s Lower-Emissions Ambitions

New world-scale blue hydrogen facility plans to fuel operations and reduce emissions.

Custom Content by ExxonMobil

There’s a buzz in Baytown, Texas. The city just outside of Houston is already home to one of ExxonMobil’s largest integrated refining and chemical complexes.

Blue hydrogen is a low-carbon fuel produced from natural gas combined with carbon capture and storage (CCS). This clean-burning fuel produces zero CO₂ emissions when combusted.

Combining hydrogen with carbon capture and storage supports ExxonMobil’s larger ambition of reaching net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2050.

Once completed, the facility could produce up to 1 billion cubic feet of blue hydrogen per day while capturing up to 10 million metric tons of CO₂ annually.

Fueling the Baytown complex with hydrogen would significantly reduce emissions across its manufacturing operations.
